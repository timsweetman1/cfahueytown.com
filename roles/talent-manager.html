6-month success definition

At 6 months, you have built a steady hiring engine that brings in great people and helps them start strong. You consistently:

  • Attract qualified applicants through consistent outreach and recruiting.
  • Run a respectful, efficient interview and selection process.
  • Improve new-hire readiness through clear onboarding coordination.
  • Build relationships with schools, community partners, and referrals.
  • Protect the team culture by hiring for character, coachability, and reliability.

Primary objectives (next 6 months)

Build a reliable pipeline of applicants

Outcome: We consistently have qualified candidates in process.

Measured by (public metrics):

  • Steady flow of applicants week to week
  • Strong recruiting presence in the community
  • Healthy mix of sources (referrals, schools, online, events)

Improve speed and quality of hiring decisions

Outcome: Great candidates move quickly from interest to offer.

Measured by (public metrics):

  • Faster time from application to interview
  • Clear selection criteria and consistent interview process
  • Fewer “no-show” or drop-off moments due to poor follow-up

Improve the first 30 days for new hires

Outcome: New Team Members start confident and supported.

Measured by (public metrics):

  • Clear onboarding steps communicated before Day 1
  • New hires know what to expect and who to ask for help
  • Strong early engagement and attendance reliability

Strengthen culture through hiring standards

Outcome: We hire people who fit the expectations and raise the bar.

Measured by (public metrics):

  • Consistent evaluation of coachability, professionalism, and teamwork
  • Strong feedback loop with operations leaders on new-hire performance
  • Improved retention and reliability over time

Key responsibilities

  • Manage recruiting pipeline: sourcing, screening, scheduling, follow-up.
  • Conduct interviews and coordinate hiring decisions.
  • Coordinate onboarding logistics and first-week readiness with leaders.
  • Build relationships with schools and community partners.
  • Maintain a professional, respectful candidate experience.

What “great” looks like (behaviors)

  • Urgency: You follow up quickly and don’t let great candidates drift.
  • Organization: You track every candidate and close loops.
  • Judgment: You hire for character and coachability, not just availability.
  • Warmth + professionalism: Candidates feel respected and informed.
  • Partnership: You align closely with operations on needs and standards.

Accountability & support

  • You’ll be supported with clear staffing priorities, interview standards, and leadership alignment.
  • Performance is evaluated by pipeline consistency, hiring quality, onboarding readiness, and the candidate experience.
